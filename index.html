Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish

"Stay hungry, stay foolish" is a phrase coined by Steve Jobs in his famous Stanford University commencement speech in 2005. The phrase encourages people to maintain a sense of curiosity and hunger for knowledge, and to never become complacent or satisfied with their current level of understanding. Jobs believed that staying foolish meant being open to new ideas and taking risks, even if it meant failing along the way. By staying hungry and foolish, individuals can continue to learn and grow throughout their lives, and can ultimately achieve their fullest potential. The phrase has become a popular mantra for those seeking to embrace innovation and creativity, and to pursue their passions with a sense of fearlessness and determination.

DO MORE.

The Apple 11

The Apple II was one of the first mass-produced and highly successful personal computers, introduced by Apple Computer Inc. in 1977. It was designed primarily by Steve Wozniak and was a significant improvement over its predecessor, the Apple I. The Apple II featured an integrated keyboard, color graphics, and support for a wide range of software and peripherals, including floppy disk drives and printers. It quickly became a popular choice for both personal and business use, and helped to establish Apple as a major player in the emerging computer industry.

iphones

The iPhone is a line of smartphones designed and sold by Apple Inc. It was first introduced in 2007 and has since become one of the most popular and recognizable brands in the smartphone market. The iPhone features a sleek and user-friendly design, advanced camera technology, and a wide range of apps and features that make it a versatile tool for both personal and professional use. With regular updates and new releases, the iPhone continues to evolve and set the standard for the modern smartphone.

iMac

The iMac is a line of all-in-one desktop computers designed and sold by Apple Inc. It was first introduced in 1998 and has since undergone several iterations and upgrades. The iMac features a sleek and minimalist design, with the computer and monitor integrated into a single unit. It also offers advanced processing power, high-resolution displays, and a wide range of software and applications, making it a popular choice for both personal and professional use. With regular updates and new releases, the iMac continues to set the standard for all-in-one desktop computers.
